Data scientists need to practice their art. They need to build tools. They need to collaborate and learn from each other. They need a sandbox where they can play together with real data, solving real problems.

Many organizations have lots of data. They just need help understanding it. They need data scientists to hack on their data to find patterns and bring insight.

DataHack is a win-win solution ...

DataHack is a one-day hackathon dedicated to solving a well-defined problem for an organization in need. The DataHack committee works with an org to define their key problem and gather the necessary data to solve it (the dirty work). Then the hackers get one day to do the analysis and come up with a solution (the fun part).

All data and code generated by DataHack is open source on GitHub. Developers can link directly to their forks and commits as a stable record of their contributions.

FAQ

I'm a budding data scientist. How can I get involved?
Fill out the hacker survey to let us know your data skills and interests. Follow us on Twitter to see what problems we're considering for the hackathon. Check the calendar for the next DataHack-athon near you.
I'm an org in need. How can you help me?
Fill out the org survey to let us know what data you have, and what you'd like to figure out from it. Someone from the DataHack committee will contact you to discuss the deets. Follow us on Twitter to get a sense for the problems we're solving.
